# Paper Summary: Hew & Cheung (2010)
**Title:** "Use of 3-D Immersive Virtual Worlds in K-12 and Higher Education Settings"
**Source:** British Journal of Educational Technology

### Key Findings:
1. **Immersion:** 3D virtual worlds serve as vital spaces for **spatial knowledge representation** and experiential learning.
2. **Systems Thinking:** Navigating and interacting with 3D objects helps students understand **complex, multi-dimensional systems** better than 2D environments.
3. **Flow State:** High-fidelity 3D environments sustain student attention for longer periods, facilitating "Deep Learning."

### Why this matters for Mentora:
Mentora uses Unity HDRP to create a world that feels real. When a student code-manipulates a 3D object (like setting `islandVisible`), they aren't just changing a boolean—they are reshaping their virtual reality, which solidifies their understanding of how code interacts with the physical world.
