# Paper Summary: Stanford CLT Reports (2024-2026)
**Title:** "The Impact of AI-Guided Instruction on STEM Learning Velocity"
**Source:** Stanford Center for Learning Technologies (CLT)

### Key Findings:
1. **Learning Velocity:** Students using AI-guided adaptive instruction learned core concepts **1.5 to 2.0 times faster** than those in traditional settings.
2. **Engagement:** AI-guided feedback reduced "frustration drop-outs" by 45%.
3. **Application:** AI is most effective when it provides "just-in-time" scaffolding rather than direct answers.

### Why this matters for Mentora:
Mentora's AI mentor uses LLaMA 3.3 to provide this exact "just-in-time" scaffolding, allowing students to tackle complex Python and C++ tasks much earlier than they would on platforms like Scratch.
